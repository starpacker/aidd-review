# BEN-2293 Phase IIa Failure — BenevolentAI, 2023

**Full citation**: BenevolentAI press release, April 5, 2023. BusinessWire, April 4, 2023.
**Section relevance**: Section 4.1 (case study: target hypothesis failure)

## Key Findings
- Phase IIa, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 91 patients (49 BEN-2293, 42 placebo)
- Ages 18-65, mild-to-moderate atopic dermatitis
- 1% topical BEN-2293, BID for 28 days, max 30% BSA
- **Primary endpoint (safety): MET** — safe and well-tolerated
- **Secondary endpoints (EASI, NRS): NOT MET** in ITT population
- Subgroup: BSA ≥20% showed EASI treatment effect (PP p=0.0427; interaction p=0.0237)
- Exact EASI/NRS numerical changes NOT disclosed

## BenevolentAI's Explanation
- CSO Dr. Anne Phelan: results "not conclusive"
- Noted "significant relationship of EASI efficacy with affected BSA requires further investigation"
- Did NOT publicly discuss compensatory pathways (JAK/STAT, IL-4/IL-13)

## Our Analysis (IMPORTANT: attribute correctly in review)
- The compensatory pathway argument is OUR editorial analysis based on AD biology, NOT BenevolentAI's stated explanation
- AD pathogenesis involves extensive pathway redundancy: IL-4, IL-13, IL-22, IL-31, TSLP, IFN-gamma → JAK-STAT
- Blocking Trk axis alone leaves dominant inflammatory pathways intact
- This analysis is well-supported by immunology literature but must be attributed as "we propose" not "the company found"

## Quotes
- "not conclusive" — BenevolentAI CSO

## Our Take
- Failure mode = target hypothesis failure. AI did the chemistry perfectly (safe molecule, confirmed target engagement) but the biological hypothesis was insufficient
- Perfect illustration of "Chemistry Problem solved, Biology Problem not"
- The subgroup signal (BSA ≥20%) is interesting but was not pursued — company laid off ~180 staff
- No peer-reviewed clinical paper published on BEN-2293 itself
