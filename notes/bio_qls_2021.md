# Clinical Development Success Rates — BIO/QLS, 2021

**Full citation**: BIO, Informa Pharma Intelligence, QLS Advisors. "Clinical Development Success Rates and Contributing Factors 2011-2020." February 17, 2021. URL: https://go.bio.org/rs/490-EHZ-999/images/ClinicalDevelopmentSuccessRates2011_2020.pdf
**Section relevance**: Sections 1, 4.1 (industry baseline for comparison)

## Key Findings
- Phase I → Phase II: 52.0%
- **Phase II → Phase III: 28.9%** (the "valley of death")
- Phase III → NDA/BLA: 57.8%
- NDA/BLA → Approval: 90.6%
- **Overall LOA (Phase I → Approval): 7.9%**
- Scale: 12,728 transitions, 9,704 programs, 1,779 companies, 2011-2020

## By Modality
- Small molecules (NMEs): 5.7% LOA
- Biologics: 9.1%
- Vaccines: 9.7%

## By Therapeutic Area
- Hematology: 26.1% (highest)
- Oncology: 5.3% (lowest)

## Critical Finding for Our Review
- Programs using **patient preselection biomarkers**: LOA = 25.9%
- Programs WITHOUT biomarkers: LOA = 8.4%
- **2x improvement with biomarker-guided development** — directly supports our argument that biology-aware approaches (understanding patient biology, not just molecular chemistry) dramatically improve outcomes

## Our Take
- THE industry baseline reference. Jayatunga's AI Phase II ~40% should be compared against this 28.9%
- The biomarker finding is powerful evidence for Section 6 (biology-aware AIDD)
- Phase II is confirmed as the single biggest bottleneck — our "Biology Problem" thesis
