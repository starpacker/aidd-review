# Pharma Attrition Rates — Kola & Landis, 2004

**Full citation**: Kola I, Landis J. "Can the pharmaceutical industry reduce attrition rates?" Nature Reviews Drug Discovery. 2004. 3:711-716. PMID: 15286737.
**Section relevance**: Section 2 (ADMET evolution), Section 4.2 (cascading failure)

## Key Findings
- In 1991: PK/bioavailability failures = **~40%** of drug development failures (single largest cause)
- By 2000: PK/bioavailability failures dropped to **~10%**
- Shift: efficacy (~30%) and safety (~30%) became dominant failure causes by 2000
- Data from 10 major pharmaceutical companies

## Methods
- Survey of large pharma companies' internal attrition data
- Compared 1991 vs. 2000 failure cause distributions

## Limitations
- Data from large pharma only (may not represent biotech)
- Self-reported by companies
- Pre-deep-learning era

## Our Take
- Foundational reference for the ADMET success story
- The 40%→10% reduction shows that focused effort CAN reduce specific attrition causes
- Implication for our review: if ADMET was solved by systematic preclinical screening, the Biology Problem could theoretically be addressed by better preclinical biology (OoC, organoids)
- Pair with Sun et al. 2022 for modern update (10-15%)
