## Case 1: BenevolentAI BEN-2293 (Pan-Trk for Atopic Dermatitis)

### Trial Design
- Phase IIa, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- 91 patients (49 BEN-2293, 42 placebo), ages 18-65, mild-to-moderate AD
- 1% topical BEN-2293, BID for 28 days, max 30% BSA
- Primary: safety/tolerability (MET)
- Secondary: EASI, pruritus NRS (NOT MET in ITT)

### Results
- Safe and well-tolerated
- No statistically significant effect on NRS or EASI in ITT
- Subgroup: BSA ≥20% → EASI treatment effect (PP p=0.0427; interaction p=0.0237)
- Exact EASI/NRS numerical scores NOT disclosed

### BenevolentAI's Explanation
- CSO Dr. Anne Phelan: results "not conclusive"
- "Significant relationship of EASI efficacy with affected BSA requires further investigation"
- Did NOT publicly discuss compensatory pathways (JAK/STAT, IL-4/IL-13)
- **CRITICAL**: The compensatory pathway argument is EXTERNAL analysis, NOT company's explanation

### Citations
- BenevolentAI press release, April 5, 2023
- BusinessWire, April 4, 2023
- No peer-reviewed clinical paper on BEN-2293

## Case 2: Exscientia EXS-21546 (A2A for Immuno-Oncology)

### Phase Ia Results (June 2022)
- 60 healthy volunteers, 3-part dose-finding
- Part 1: randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled SAD (41 subjects)
- Part 2: MAD at 150 mg BID (8 subjects)
- Part 3: 3-way crossover bioavailability (11 subjects)
- Confirmed target product profile: potency, high selectivity, low brain exposure
- No CNS AEs; one Grade 3 SAE (elevated ALT/AST)
- Dose-dependent CREB phosphorylation inhibition in CD8+ cells

### Phase I/II IGNITE Trial
- EXS-21546 + anti-PD-1 for immunotherapy-resistant RCC and NSCLC
- Up to 110 patients planned

### Discontinuation (October 2023)
- Exact quote: "Based on modelling of the clinical and preclinical data, it will be challenging for [EXS-21546] to reach a suitable therapeutic index."
- "A prolonged, high level of target coverage is necessary for therapeutic effect"
- Peer data (AstraZeneca AZD4635/imaradenant) further undermined A2A mechanism
- AZD4635: Ki 1.7 nM, limited clinical activity despite good target engagement

### Citations
- BusinessWire, June 14, 2022 (Phase 1a topline)
- BusinessWire, October 3, 2023 (pipeline prioritisation)
- AACR 2023 Abstract CT114
- AZD4635: Clinical Cancer Research 2022, PMID: 36044531, DOI: 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-22-0986

## Case 3: Recursion REC-994 / Tempol (Cerebral Cavernous Malformation)

### SYCAMORE Trial Design
- Phase II, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- 62 patients (enrolled June 2023), 3 arms: 200mg, 400mg, placebo
- Per-arm: 400mg (N=20), 200mg (N=17), placebo (N=18)
- 12 months + long-term open-label extension
- Primary: safety/tolerability (MET)
- Secondary: MRI lesion volume, clinician/patient-reported outcomes, biomarkers
- NOT powered for statistical significance (signal-finding)

### 12-Month MRI Results (Feb 2025, ISC)
- 400mg: mean -457 mm³; 50% showed reduction vs. 28% placebo
- 200mg: mean +61 mm³ (similar to placebo)
- Placebo: mean +53 mm³
- p-values: 200mg vs placebo p=0.987; 400mg vs placebo **p=0.449 (NOT significant)**
- Hemosiderin ring: trend toward reduction at 400mg

### Patient-Reported Outcomes
- mRS: 400mg trends toward improvement vs. placebo decline
- PROMIS29, CCM-HI, NIHSS, SMSS, CGI, PGI: "did not demonstrate differences"

### Long-Term Extension FAILURE
- Crossover (placebo→400mg): NO promising trends
- 400mg→400mg: NOT sustained, **indistinguishable from natural history**
- Initial 12-month signal = likely statistical artifact in underpowered study

### Discontinuation (May 2025)
- Recursion halted 4 programs; $464M net loss in 2024

### Citations
- GlobeNewswire, September 3, 2024 (Phase 2 announcement)
- GlobeNewswire, February 5, 2025 (ISC presentation)
- GEN News, May 2025 (pipeline restructuring)

## Case 4: Insilico Medicine ISM001-055 / Rentosertib (IPF)

### Nature Medicine Publication
- Ren et al., Nature Medicine, June 2025. DOI: 10.1038/s41591-025-03743-2. PMID: 40461817.

### GENESIS-IPF Trial Design
- Phase IIa, multicenter, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled
- 71 patients, 22 sites in China, 12 weeks
- 4 arms: 30mg QD (n=18), 30mg BID (n=18), 60mg QD (n=18), placebo (n=17)
- Primary: safety/tolerability (MET)
- Secondary: FVC change

### FVC Results
| Arm | FVC Change | 95% CI |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| 60mg QD | **+98.4 mL** | 10.9 to 185.9 |
| 30mg BID | +19.7 mL | — |
| 30mg QD | -27 mL | — |
| Placebo | **-20.3 mL** | -116.1 to 75.6 |

⚠️ **CORRECTION**: Earlier notes stated placebo = -62.3 mL. Actual = **-20.3 mL**.
Delta vs. placebo = +118.7 mL (still clinically meaningful).

### Statistical Significance
- No formal p-values reported
- 95% CI for 60mg QD excludes zero → nominal significance
- NOT powered as pivotal efficacy trial

### Phase IIb/III Plans
- "Begun discussions with regulatory authorities"
- No specific timeline, geography, or design announced

## Case 5: Zasocitinib / TAK-279 (TYK2 for Plaque Psoriasis)

### Phase 2 (JAMA 2024)
- Fensome A et al., JAMA 2024. PMID: 39167366. DOI: 10.1001/jama.2024.15531
- 259 patients, 5 arms, 12 weeks
- PASI 75 week 12: placebo 6%, 2mg 18%, 5mg 44%, 15mg 68%, 30mg 67%
- PASI 90: 15mg 45%, 30mg 46%
- PASI 100: 15mg 15%, 30mg 33%

### Phase 3 LATITUDE (Dec 2025)
- Two pivotal: 3001 (n=693) + 3002 (n=1,108), 21 countries
- Active comparator: apremilast
- PASI 75 week 16: zasocitinib 61.3%/51.9% vs. placebo 5.0%/4.0% vs. apremilast 16.8%/15.9%
- PASI 100 week 16: 33.4%/25.2% vs. placebo 0.7%/1.1% vs. apremilast 2.9%/4.3%
- Met ALL co-primary + ALL 44 ranked secondary endpoints
- NDA expected FY2026

### AI Methodology
- Nimbus + Schrodinger collaboration (since 2009)
- Free Energy Perturbation (FEP+): quantum mechanical simulations + ML
- 4,000 candidates enumerated, scored for potency/selectivity/solubility
- 1.7 million-fold selectivity TYK2 over JAK1 via JH2 pseudokinase domain
- Target + scaffold chosen by humans from BMS literature (deucravacitinib)
- = "AI-assisted optimization" NOT "AI-originated discovery"
