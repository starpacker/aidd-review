## Key Findings — Phase 2 (JAMA 2024) — UPDATED from PMC full text
- **Design**: "Phase 2b, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multiple-dose"
- **Patients**: "287 patients randomized, 259 (90.2%) received at least 1 dose"
- **Arms**: "1:1:1:1:1 to receive zasocitinib at 2, 5, 15, or 30 mg or placebo orally, once daily"
- **Duration**: "12-week treatment period and a 4-week follow-up period"
- **Primary endpoint**: "Proportion of patients achieving 75% or greater improvement in PASI score (PASI 75) at week 12"
- PASI 75 at week 12: placebo 6% (3 pts), 2mg 18% (9 pts), 5mg 44% (23 pts), 15mg 68% (36 pts), 30mg 67% (35 pts)
- PASI 90: highest dose 46% (24 pts)
- PASI 100: 30mg 33% (17 pts)
- **Quality of life**: "Patients receiving zasocitinib, 30 mg, had the greatest reduction in DLQI"

### Safety Data (from PMC full text)
- "TEAEs occurred for 23 patients (44%) receiving placebo and 28 (53%) to 31 (62%) patients receiving the 4 different doses"
- Most frequent: COVID-19 (12%), acne/acneiform dermatitis (2-6%), diarrhea (2-6%)
- "No dose dependency" in AEs
- "No MACE, thromboembolic events, or opportunistic infections"
- Serious AE: one patient with "severe pleural effusion and severe pericardial effusion" (considered unrelated)
- Creatine kinase elevations observed but "no cases of rhabdomyolysis"
- Study discontinuations: "1 to 2 patients (2%-4%)" in zasocitinib groups

### Comparison with Deucravacitinib
- "Although identical in mechanism of action to deucravacitinib, zasocitinib demonstrates a higher level of selectivity than deucravacitinib for the JH2 domain"
- "Deucravacitinib binds with a lower binding affinity for TYK2 than that demonstrated by zasocitinib"
- "Whether these differences translate into corresponding levels of clinical efficacy remains to be confirmed in further clinical trials"

## Key Findings — Phase 3 LATITUDE (Dec 2025)
- Two pivotal studies: 3001 (n=693) and 3002 (n=1,108), 21 countries
- Active comparator: apremilast (Otezla)
- PASI 75 at week 16: zasocitinib 61.3%/51.9% vs. placebo 5.0%/4.0% vs. apremilast 16.8%/15.9%
- PASI 100 at week 16: 33.4%/25.2% vs. placebo 0.7%/1.1% vs. apremilast 2.9%/4.3%
- Met ALL co-primary and ALL 44 ranked secondary endpoints
- NDA expected FY2026

## AI Methodology
- Nimbus + Schrodinger collaboration (since 2009)
- Used Free Energy Perturbation (FEP+): quantum mechanical simulations + ML
- Enumerated 4,000 candidates, scored for potency, selectivity, solubility
- Achieved 1.7 million-fold selectivity for TYK2 over JAK1 via JH2 pseudokinase domain
- Target (TYK2 JH2) and chemical scaffold chosen by humans based on BMS literature (deucravacitinib)

## The AI Attribution Debate
- Physics-based simulation + ML, NOT generative AI / deep learning
- Target selection, scaffold, and design strategy came from human researchers
- AI/computation accelerated optimization, did not originate the molecule de novo
- Spectrum: "AI-assisted optimization" (zasocitinib) vs. "AI-originated discovery" (Rentosertib)
- Must discuss this spectrum honestly in Section 7

## Our Take
- If approved, could be first "AI-assisted" FDA approval — but the AI attribution is genuinely debatable
- Useful in Section 3 as a success but with explicit caveat about what "AI-designed" means
- Key point for Section 7: even if zasocitinib gains approval, our thesis about the Biology Problem holds — psoriasis is a well-understood indication with validated targets. The real test is novel indications
